Abstract

Existing 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) frameworks rely on camera-specific rasterization, suffering from inconsistent solid-angle sampling and degraded performance across heterogeneous camera models, including perspective, fisheye, and omnidirectional cameras. UniTriSplat reformulates Gaussian splatting on the unit sphere through HEALPix discretization. Its equal-area spherical grid is aligned with the angular resolution of input images, while forward rendering and gradient propagation are derived directly in the spherical radian domain. This produces uniform optimization behavior from narrow-FoV images to full 360-degree panoramas. A HEALPix-aware SSIM loss further respects spherical neighborhood structure. Across diverse camera models, UniTriSplat improves cross-camera generalization while preserving geometric fidelity and rendering quality.

Method

One spherical rasterizer, universal camera models

UniTriSplat decouples the Gaussian representation from camera-specific image planes by rasterizing directly onto an area-uniform HEALPix sphere.

Experiments

Reconstruction and cross-camera rendering

UniTriSplat preserves fine structures across the viewing sphere and maintains consistent scene geometry when switching camera models.
